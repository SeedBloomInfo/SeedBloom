Mission Statement

SeedBloom Foundation nurtures hidden Jewish roots — especially those buried under Soviet silence — like seeds never watered. We help provide warmth, guidance, and nourishment so they can grow, thrive, and proudly blossom.

Vision Statement

A world where every Jewish soul — no matter how hidden, or seemingly distant — has the opportunity to grow, connect, and flourish. We believe that identity, when nurtured gently and meaningfully, can bloom into pride, purpose, and belonging.

Hebrew name: Zera U’Prichah

Why SeedBloom Was Created

For decades, Jewish life in the Soviet Union was buried — not destroyed, but hidden. Generations grew up without the chance to learn, to ask, to believe, to belong.

But the spark never fully disappeared.

In one city — far from centers of Jewish life — someone met a young man who had never heard of Shabbat, never seen a mezuzah, never been told he was part of something ancient and beautiful. They spoke. They studied. They lit candles.

Then another person. And another. Each one was a seed — buried, but alive. Waiting.

This work began quietly. No big budgets. No global spotlight. Just one-on-one care. A cup of tea. A ride to the hospital. A Jewish book handed gently to someone who never knew they could call themselves Jewish.

As years passed, the need only grew — not for flashy programs or big buildings, but for people who would walk with others, listen, teach, and help them bloom.

SeedBloom was created to ensure this kind of care could grow roots, survive, and continue — even if the circumstances changed. It's about people. The ones finding their identity. And the ones helping them do it.

Who We Help

SeedBloom Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in the United States.

We support the personal, spiritual, and humanitarian care of Jewish individuals — especially in areas where Jewish identity was suppressed or lost, such as Eastern Europe.

Our work centers on people — helping each soul find its path back to growth through:

  • Gentle relationships
  • Jewish education
  • Cultural renewal
  • Direct personal care
